# Game-Regulation Source Map

This file is a compact source category map. It is not a cached law database.
Prefer live official sources when researching.

## Source Priorities

1. Statutes and regulations.
2. Official regulator guidance and decisions.
3. Official court or agency decisions.
4. Official explanatory notes and press releases.
5. Practitioner or academic commentary that cites primary law.

## Korea

Potential source categories:

- game industry statutes and enforcement rules
- game rating and content classification guidance
- consumer protection statutes and regulator guidance
- advertising and display rules
- youth protection materials
- official agency notices and enforcement releases
- fair trade and app-market rules where platform/payment facts require them
- copyright/content industry sources where IP or digital-content issues arise

Use `korean-law` MCP when available. If unavailable, record
`mcp_unavailable` or `partial_sources` when material.

Korean source planning checklist:

1. Identify the primary regulator: GRAC, MCST, KFTC, KCC, PIPC, FSC, or another
   body.
2. Fetch current statute and delegated rules.
3. Check supplementary provisions and amendment history.
4. Check official notices/guidance for procedure and forms.
5. Check enforcement releases or decisions if the question concerns sanctions.
6. Treat self-regulatory association materials as non-primary unless officially
   incorporated.

## Japan

Potential source categories:

- consumer affairs rules
- online game and digital item guidance
- prize/advertising rules
- payment services and virtual currency rules where relevant
- app platform compliance sources where legally relevant

Confirm with official Japanese government or regulator sources where possible.

Japan source planning checklist:

1. Distinguish statutory consumer/prize rules from industry self-regulation.
2. Verify any gacha/kompu-gacha point against Consumer Affairs Agency materials
   or current official guidance.
3. Treat JOGA/CERO materials as self-regulatory unless an official source gives
   them legal effect.

## EU

Potential source categories:

- consumer protection directives and national implementation
- unfair commercial practices
- digital services/platform rules
- advertising and influencer marketing rules
- youth and minor protection rules

For privacy issues, defer detailed GDPR analysis to the relevant specialist when
one is co-running.

EU source planning checklist:

1. Determine whether the issue is EU-level or member-state-level.
2. For loot boxes/gambling, identify national regulator positions.
3. For consumer and platform issues, distinguish directives/regulations from
   national implementation.
4. Do not state "EU bans/allows" where member-state law controls.

## United States

Potential source categories:

- FTC consumer protection and advertising guidance
- state consumer protection rules
- state loot box or gambling-adjacent materials where relevant
- platform/app-store rules only when legally relevant to the answer

State-level variation should be recorded as a coverage gap unless the question
specifies a state.

US source planning checklist:

1. Separate federal consumer protection from state gambling/consumer rules.
2. Treat ESRB and platform rules as self-regulatory/commercial unless linked to
   legal obligations.
3. Flag state variation where the question does not specify states.
