# Judgment Summary Reference Pack

Detailed judgment and final-order summary framework for
`skills/specialists/judgment-summary.md`. Load this pack when preparing,
tailoring, or quality-checking a judgment summary, appellate disposition brief,
or final-order summary.

This pack is a structure and quality-control reference. It does not replace the
judgment, order, or opinion text.

## Quick Start

1. Identify the judgment, court, procedural posture, and final disposition.
2. Extract standards of review and issue-by-issue holdings.
3. Track precedent treatment and separate majority, concurrence, and dissent.
4. Capture remand instructions, deadlines, costs, fees, and conditions.
5. Translate the disposition into practical implications without advocacy.
6. Verify that every holding and material fact has a pinpoint citation.

## Procedural History

Capture:

- complaint, motion, trial, administrative, or lower-court posture;
- issues preserved or waived;
- lower-court disposition;
- basis for appeal or review;
- scope of remand from any prior appeal;
- current procedural endpoint.

Do not infer procedural steps from docket context unless the source supports
them.

## Practical Implications

Frame implications neutrally:

| Audience | Implication |
|---|---|
| Litigation | Effect on pleading, evidence, remedies, appeal, settlement, or remand strategy. |
| Compliance | Effect on policies, controls, disclosures, monitoring, or regulator posture. |
| Transactional | Effect on contract drafting, risk allocation, diligence, or closing conditions. |
| Research | Effect on doctrine, precedent, split, or open questions. |

Rules:

- Keep implications tied to holdings or remedies.
- Avoid advocacy framing.
- Label uncertain implications as such.

## Output Quality Checklist

- [ ] Metadata and procedural history are complete or marked `Not stated`.
- [ ] Standards of review are captured when relevant.
- [ ] Holdings are issue-specific and pinpointed.
- [ ] Disposition terms are exact.
- [ ] Remand instructions and deadlines are not omitted.
- [ ] Precedent treatment is separated from general reasoning.
- [ ] Concurrence and dissent are not treated as controlling unless justified.
- [ ] Practical implications trace to holdings or remedies.
- [ ] The summary does not infer facts absent from the judgment.

## Pitfalls

- Treating dicta as an alternative holding.
- Stating "reversed" without explaining whether the judgment was also vacated
  or remanded.
- Omitting fee, cost, injunction, or compliance conditions.
- Collapsing standard of review into the merits standard.
- Treating settlement orders as precedential judgments.
- Giving strategy advice without grounding it in the disposition.
- Ignoring appeal status when finality matters.
