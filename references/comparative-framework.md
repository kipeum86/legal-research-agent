# Comparative Analysis Framework (Mode B)

When producing Mode B (Comparative Matrix) output, use this framework to ensure consistent, apples-to-apples comparison across jurisdictions.

## Standard Comparison Axes

Every Mode B deliverable must compare jurisdictions along these axes. Not all axes apply to every query — mark inapplicable axes as "N/A" rather than omitting them.

| # | Axis | Description | Example |
|---|------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | **Regulatory Scope** | What conduct, entities, or transactions does the law cover? | "All data controllers processing personal data of residents" |
| 2 | **Obligated Parties** | Who bears the compliance obligation? | "Data controller and processor jointly" |
| 3 | **Key Obligations** | Core duties imposed by the law | "Consent, DPIA, breach notification within 72h" |
| 4 | **Exemptions & Safe Harbors** | Carve-outs, thresholds, or de minimis rules | "SMEs with < 250 employees exempt from records obligation" |
| 5 | **Sanctions & Penalties** | Maximum penalties, penalty types, enforcement track record | "Up to 4% of global annual revenue or EUR 20M" |
| 6 | **Competent Authority** | Regulator(s) with enforcement power | "PIPC (Korea), CNIL (France)" |
| 7 | **Effective Date & Transition** | When the law took effect; any transitional periods | "Enacted 2023-03-14, 6-month grace period" |
| 8 | **Extraterritorial Reach** | Does the law apply to foreign entities? Under what conditions? | "Applies to foreign entities targeting domestic residents" |
| 9 | **Cross-Border Mechanisms** | Transfer requirements, mutual recognition, adequacy | "Adequacy decision, SCCs, BCRs" |
| 10 | **Pending Reforms** | Active legislative proposals, regulatory guidance in progress | "Amendment bill pending in committee since 2025-09" |

## Divergence Commentary Rules

After the matrix, include a **Divergence Commentary** section that:

1. **Highlights material differences** — where jurisdictions take substantively different approaches.
2. **Explains the practical impact** — what the divergence means for a party operating across these jurisdictions.
3. **Identifies the strictest standard** — which jurisdiction imposes the highest compliance burden on each axis.
4. **Notes convergence trends** — where jurisdictions are moving toward alignment (e.g., EU adequacy, mutual recognition).

## Practical Implications Section (Mandatory)

Every Mode B output must end with a **Practical Implications** section:

```markdown
## Practical Implications

### Compliance Strategy
- [Which jurisdiction's standard should serve as the compliance baseline and why]
- [Key actions required to meet multi-jurisdiction obligations]

### Risk Hotspots
- [Where divergence creates the highest compliance risk]
- [Where enforcement is most active]

### Monitoring Points
- [Pending reforms that could change the analysis]
- [Regulatory guidance expected]
```

## Extended Axes (Optional)

Add these axes when relevant to the specific query:

| Axis | When to add |
|------|-------------|
| **Licensing / Registration** | Market entry, regulated industries |
| **Consumer Protection** | B2C, platform, e-commerce queries |
| **Local Representation** | Foreign entity operating locally |
| **Record-Keeping / Reporting** | Compliance program design |
| **Private Right of Action** | Litigation risk assessment |
| **Industry Self-Regulation** | Where industry codes supplement legislation |
